The Julian Assange instinct: bring down society in the name of liberty! But no liberal society was built on radial liberalism. Structure is what protects and affords freedom - in so far as people actually want freedom. (Most really prefer security; liberalism is the luxury of a civil society that is necessarily constructed and maintained by arguably illiberal means). Esp. freedom of conscience, the confidence that you can say what you want in private. This is something he himself claims, e.g. on Today programme "I am a private person".

There is, in much political thought, an underlying resentment of the world. A dissatisfaction with what's there, what's given. Truth; Nature; whatever you want to call it. Whenever someone enthuses about "radical change", you can be sure that he thinks he really means it; let's turn the world upside-down! burn it all and start again!

Why? As if it'd end up any different.
